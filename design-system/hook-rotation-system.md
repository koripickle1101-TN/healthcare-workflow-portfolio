# LinkedIn Hook Rotation System

Created by Kori Pickle

## Purpose

Use rotating hook styles to keep healthcare operations, RCM, risk management, patient safety, and workflow content from sounding repetitive.

## Hook Type 1: Contrarian

Use when challenging common assumptions.

Examples:

Most near misses are not employee failures. They are workflow warning signals.

Risk management is not paperwork. It is prevention before harm reaches the patient.

A clean claim does not start in billing. It starts upstream.

Denials are not only billing problems. Many start in intake, documentation, eligibility, or authorization.

## Hook Type 2: Data Driven

Use when discussing measurable performance.

Examples:

If near-miss reporting is low, that does not always mean risk is low.

A rising denial rate is often a workflow visibility problem before it becomes a revenue problem.

If A/R days keep increasing, the issue may have started before the claim was ever submitted.

Low error reporting can mean safety, but it can also mean fear.

## Hook Type 3: Authority Statement

Use when positioning yourself as a healthcare operations thinker.

Examples:

Strong healthcare leaders do not blame first. They investigate the workflow first.

The best RCM teams do not only chase denials. They prevent upstream failure.

Patient safety improves when leaders treat near misses as intelligence, not inconvenience.

Healthcare workflows break quietly before performance metrics expose the damage.

## Hook Type 4: Question Based

Use when inviting discussion.

Examples:

What happens when staff stop trusting the reporting system?

How many workflow failures are being hidden because staff are afraid to speak up?

What if the biggest revenue cycle problem is not billing, but intake?

How can healthcare leaders protect accountability without making reporting feel dangerous?

## Hook Type 5: Patient-to-Professional Perspective

Use when connecting patient experience to operational insight.

Examples:

From the patient side, a delay feels personal. From the operations side, it often starts as a workflow gap.

Patients feel the impact of workflow failures long before leaders see the metric.

A broken handoff does not just slow down a process. It changes the patient experience.

## Rule

Do not use the same hook type twice in a row if posting weekly.

Rotate hooks to build authority without sounding repetitive.
