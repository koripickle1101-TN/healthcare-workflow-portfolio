# LinkedIn Brand System: Feminine Executive Healthcare Operations Identity

Created by Kori Pickle

## Core Identity

This brand system is designed for healthcare operations, revenue cycle management, workflow integrity, risk management, patient safety analysis, and portfolio-backed LinkedIn authority building.

The visual identity should feel premium, executive, editorial, feminine, clean, strategic, and credible.

## Primary Brand Colors

Background: Pure White #FFFFFF
Accent Color: Tennessee Orange #FF8200
Font Color: Black #111111
Secondary Text: Deep Gray #333333
Structure Color: Warm Gray #E8E2DC
Soft Neutral: Champagne Gray #F6EDE7

## Typography Direction

### Headlines

Use high-contrast editorial serif headlines.

Headline direction:

- Large scale
- Minimal wording
- Strong authority
- Premium editorial rhythm
- High contrast between thick and thin strokes

Recommended free font direction:

- Playfair Display
- Cormorant Garamond
- Libre Baskerville

### Supporting Text

Use clean sans-serif supporting text.

Supporting text direction:

- Simple
- Structured
- Precise
- Easy to scan
- Reduced by at least 30 percent

Recommended free font direction:

- Inter
- Source Sans 3
- Open Sans
- Lato

## Layout Rules

Use 40 to 50 percent whitespace.

Use intentional asymmetry.

Use a structured grid but avoid making the layout feel rigid.

Every slide should feel like an executive briefing, not a student assignment.

Avoid paragraph dumping.

One slide equals one idea.

## Visual Components

Use:

- Double-ring circular nodes
- Thin Tennessee Orange outer strokes
- Soft interior node glow
- Dotted connector lines
- Fading workflow lines
- Warm gray structure lines
- Minimal LinkedIn and GitHub glyphs
- Thin orange divider lines
- Editorial spacing

Avoid:

- Stock-photo medical imagery
- Cluttered icons
- Heavy blocks of text
- Generic corporate blue
- Loud gradients
- School-project formatting

## Visual Standard

The final design should look like a premium healthcare operations insight created by an emerging RCM and workflow analyst.

It should not look like a class assignment.

It should not look like generic Canva healthcare content.

It should look like a polished executive strategy asset.
