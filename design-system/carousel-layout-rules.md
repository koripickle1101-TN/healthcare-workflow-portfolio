# Carousel Layout Rules

Created by Kori Pickle

## Purpose

These rules convert healthcare operations, RCM, risk management, and patient safety insights into premium LinkedIn carousel assets.

## Slide Standard

Each slide should communicate one clear executive insight.

Do not overload the slide.

Do not explain everything visually.

Use the slide to create attention and the caption or case study to provide depth.

## Required Visual Identity

Background: Pure White #FFFFFF
Accent: Tennessee Orange #FF8200
Text: Black #111111
Secondary Text: Deep Gray #333333
Structure Lines: Warm Gray #E8E2DC

## Composition Rules

Use 40 to 50 percent whitespace.

Use intentional asymmetry.

Place the main headline large, usually left-aligned or slightly off-center.

Use thin Tennessee Orange dividers for hierarchy.

Use warm gray workflow structures in the background at low contrast.

Use double-ring nodes as visual anchors.

Use dotted or fading connector lines to suggest workflow movement.

## Slide Types

### Cover Slide

Purpose: Stop the scroll.

Use:

- Large editorial serif headline
- Short strategic subtitle
- Minimal node architecture on one side
- Signature footer

### Problem Slide

Purpose: Name the hidden failure.

Use:

- Large problem statement
- One small supporting line
- Broken connector line or isolated node

### Example Slide

Purpose: Make the concept concrete.

Use:

- Short real-world example
- One small workflow map
- Minimal icons only if necessary

### Contrast Slide

Purpose: Compare weak versus strong leadership or broken versus improved workflow.

Use:

- Two-column layout
- Warm gray divider
- Orange highlight for the stronger side

### Workflow Slide

Purpose: Show the improved process.

Use:

- 5 to 7 nodes maximum
- Dotted connector lines
- Minimal labels
- Strong spacing

### Closing Slide

Purpose: Leave a strategic question.

Use:

- Large question
- Very short framing line
- Signature footer

## Quality Check

Before exporting, ask:

- Does the slide have one idea only?
- Is there enough whitespace?
- Does it look premium and editorial?
- Does it avoid generic healthcare imagery?
- Could this fit in a LinkedIn Featured portfolio section?
- Would a recruiter or healthcare operations leader understand the point within five seconds?
