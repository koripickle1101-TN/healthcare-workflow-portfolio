# Revenue Cycle Workflow Breakdown

**Project Type:** Self-directed learning project  
**Focus Area:** Revenue Cycle Management, patient access, claims readiness, denial prevention  
**Brand System:** White background, black text, Vols Orange `#FF8200` accents

---

## Project Summary

This self-directed project examines how errors can move through a healthcare revenue cycle workflow before a claim is submitted. The goal is not to claim professional client work, but to demonstrate practical understanding of how front-end administrative processes affect downstream claim outcomes.

The project focuses on the connection between patient intake, eligibility verification, authorization alignment, documentation completeness, and claim submission readiness.

---

## Problem Statement

Revenue cycle breakdowns often appear at the billing or denial stage, but many of the root causes begin earlier in the workflow.

Common upstream risk points include:

- Incomplete patient demographic information
- Incorrect or outdated insurance details
- Missing eligibility verification
- Authorization requirements not confirmed before service
- Documentation that does not fully support the claim
- Weak handoffs between patient access, clinical documentation, and billing

These issues can create downstream rework, delayed reimbursement, avoidable denials, and patient confusion.

```text

   ↓

   ↓

   ↓
Authorization Review
   ↓
Documentation Check
   ↓
Claim Preparation
   ↓
Claim Submission
   ↓
Payment / Denial / Follow-Up
```

---

## Key Workflow Risks

| Workflow Stage | Potential Breakdown | Downstream Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Patient Intake | Missing demographics or incorrect patient information | Rework, claim edits, patient contact delays |
| Insurance Capture | Wrong payer, inactive coverage, outdated plan | Eligibility denial risk |
| Eligibility Verification | No validation before service | Preventable claim rejection or denial |
| Authorization Review | Authorization not required/required status unclear | Authorization denial risk |
| Documentation Check | Missing support for service billed | Documentation-related denial risk |
| Claim Preparation | Claim submitted without final validation | Increased denial and rework risk |

---

## Proposed Validation Checkpoints

This project proposes simple validation checkpoints before claim submission:

1. Confirm complete patient demographics.
2. Verify active insurance coverage.
3. Confirm payer-specific authorization requirements.
4. Check that documentation supports the service.
5. Review claim readiness before submission.
6. Track denial patterns and send feedback upstream.

---

## RCM Insight

The strongest revenue cycle improvements often happen before the claim is submitted.

A denial prevention mindset asks:

- What allowed the error to move forward?
- Which step should have caught it earlier?
- How can the workflow prevent the same issue next time?
