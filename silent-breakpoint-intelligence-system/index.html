The most expensive healthcare problems rarely start in billing.

They start upstream in intake documentation authorization handoffs technical execution and invisible workload. Billing is where failure arrives. It is not always where failure begins.

Core Model

Where systems break silently

Patient Intake

Incomplete demographics eligibility gaps insurance mismatch and missing front end data.

Clinical Documentation

Missing specificity undercoded encounters incomplete notes and medical necessity gaps.

Authorization Workflow

Prior authorization delays aging queues unclear ownership and no escalation trigger.

Workflow Handoffs

Siloed teams missing accountability unclear transfer points and open loop communication.

Technical Execution

Duplicated entry system mismatch manual workarounds and inconsistent data capture.

Operational Visibility

Leadership cannot manage problems that dashboards do not surface until it is too late.

Scoring Model

Silent Breakpoint Score

Each issue is scored across patient impact revenue impact staff burden repeat risk and visibility gap. The total score helps prioritize what needs attention first.

5 to 9 Low Friction

10 to 14 Moderate Breakpoint

15 to 19 High Risk Breakpoint

20 to 25 Critical Silent Failure

Sample Use Case

Prior Authorization Delay

A request remains unresolved because no one owns the aging queue after submission. The problem becomes visible when care is delayed and reimbursement risk increases. The fix is an authorization aging tracker with assigned ownership escalation rules and service date prioritization.
