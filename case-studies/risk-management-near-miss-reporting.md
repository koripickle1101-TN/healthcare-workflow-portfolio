# Risk Management Near-Miss Reporting Workflow

Created by Kori Pickle

## Project Focus

This case study explains how healthcare leaders can use near-miss reporting to improve patient safety, strengthen risk management, and support a fair accountability culture.

## Core Problem

Near misses are often underreported because staff may fear blame, embarrassment, or punishment.

When near misses are not reported, healthcare leaders lose visibility into workflow risks before those risks become patient harm events.

## Operational Example

A nurse almost gives the wrong medication because two medications have similar packaging and similar names.

The barcode scanner catches the issue before the medication reaches the patient.

Although no patient harm occurs, the event reveals a system weakness.

## Workflow Failure Points

- Look-alike medication packaging
- Sound-alike medication names
- Interruptions during medication administration
- High workload
- Potential alert fatigue
- Storage or labeling problems
- Weak feedback loop after reporting

## Leadership Risk

If leaders respond with blame first, staff may stop reporting near misses.

This creates hidden risk.

The organization may look safer on paper while actual workflow risk continues to grow.

## Improved Workflow

1. Staff reports the near miss.
2. Leader thanks the employee for speaking up.
3. Event is reviewed through a just culture framework.
4. Human error is separated from reckless behavior.
5. System causes are identified.
6. Workflow improvements are implemented.
7. Staff are informed of what changed.
8. Leaders track whether near misses decrease over time.

## Quality Management Connection

Risk management identifies what could go wrong.

Quality management improves the process so the same issue is less likely to happen again.

Together, they support safer care, stronger staff trust, and better organizational learning.

## Recommended Metrics

- Near-miss reporting volume
- Medication near-miss rate
- Repeat event patterns
- Time from report to review
- Time from review to corrective action
- Staff perception of reporting safety
- Medication scanning compliance
- Alert override frequency

## Leadership Insight

The best safety cultures do not remove accountability.

They make accountability fair, specific, and focused on learning.

### Slide 1: Cover

NEAR MISSES ARE WORKFLOW WARNING SIGNALS

### Slide 2: Contrarian Framing

A near miss is not a small event.

It is an early signal that the system almost failed.

No harm does not mean no risk.

### Slide 3: Operational Example

A nurse almost gives the wrong medication.

The packages look alike. The names sound alike. The unit is busy. The scanner catches the issue.

The patient is safe, but the workflow still needs attention.

### Slide 4: Leadership Contrast

Weak leadership asks: Who made the mistake?

Strong leadership asks: How did the system allow this to almost happen?

### Slide 5: Just Culture Balance

Human error. At-risk behavior. Reckless behavior.

The goal is not automatic punishment.

The goal is fair accountability.
