## Carousel Name

Near Misses Are Workflow Warning Signals

## Slide 1: Cover

NEAR MISSES
ARE WORKFLOW
WARNING SIGNALS

Risk management is not paperwork.
It is prevention before harm reaches the patient.

Created by Kori Pickle

## Slide 2: Contrarian Framing

A near miss is not a small event.

It is an early signal that the system almost failed.

No harm does not mean no risk.

## Slide 3: The Real Example

A clinical workflow almost breaks.

The issue is caught before it reaches the patient.

The outcome is safe.

But the workflow still needs attention.

## Slide 4: Weak Leadership vs Strong Leadership

Weak leadership asks:

Who made the mistake?

Strong leadership asks:

How did the system allow this to almost happen?

## Slide 5: The Just Culture Balance

Just culture separates:

Human error
At-risk behavior
Reckless behavior

The goal is not automatic punishment.

The goal is fair accountability.

## Slide 6: The Better Workflow

Report the near miss.
Identify what almost failed.
Look for system causes.
Fix the process.
Close the loop with staff.
Track whether risk decreases.

## Slide 7: Closing Question

Staff will not report what they fear will be used against them.

The strongest safety cultures protect both:

Honesty
Accountability

Question:

How can leaders create both?
